I believe in dance as a way to explore and play with the experience of existing.

A medium for expressing feelings and thoughts.

A physical invitation to reflection.

A primal force.

A wild language.

A community.

A seriously fun practice.



I believe in dance as radical resistance and hope.

And also as part of our popular culture -

a mirror of our past,

our present,

and with finesse and hard work,

a creator of the best of utopias.

ABOUT


Matilda Bilberg (she/her) was born and raised in Västerås and now lives in Stockholm Sweden.


Since 2013 she has been active in the field of dance and choreography both in Sweden and internationally. As a dancer, she has collaborated with choreographers and artists including Cristina Caprioli / ccap, Helena Franzén, Nicole Neidert, Livekonstkollektivet MELO, Ionnalee, David Zambrano, Maria Ulriksson, Torbjörn Grass, Julia Kraus, Linda Forsman, HDN Studio, Emeric Rabot, Tove Skeidsvoll and her collective UTOPISISTAS (Matilda Bilberg, Nerea Gurrutxaga, Jenna Hendry, Maria Teresa Tanzarella).


Matilda's own choreographic works have been presented at venues such as Dansens Hus, Västerås Konserthus, Tanzhaus Zürich, Théâtre Sévelin 36 and Rote Fabrik Zürich, as well as at festivals such as IDEA in Jordan, B.A.D. in the Basque Country, Les Rencontres Chorégraphiques de Casablanca in Morocco, and in an exchange during the KINANI festival in Mozambique.


Together with her longtime collaborator Jenna Hendry, they were selected for the Aerowaves Artists Twenty 2021 with their piece I U M I.


Within her artistic work she is also curating - facilitating for dance to happen, and has an ongoing practice with sculpting materials such as plaster. In parallel, she is sharing her knowledge by teaching in dance, improvisation and contemporary floor work methods nationally and internationally, for professionals and dance enthusiasts.


Matilda is educated in the contemporary dance program at the Royal Swedish Ballet School and holds a master's degree in Fine Arts with specialization in Choreography from Stockholm University of the Arts. Matilda has studied sports psychology and is a Thai Massage practitioner, educated at the Thai Massage School of Chiang Mai, Thailand.

By Your Neighbour- performance in Västerås Sweden

11.07.2025

In By Your Neighbour, a group of young dancers from Västerås, move through the city in an interplay between bodies, architecture, and the invisible threads that bind us together. They explore what it means to be together—to share space, time, and direction.

How can we meet in movement, listen through our bodies, and let dance become our common language beyond words?

With sensitivity and courage, they allow dance to blur boundaries—between the individual and the collective, between me and you, between the familiar and the unknown.

Welcome to experience a living choreography where every step creates new meaning.



Choreography: Matilda Bilberg, Nassim Meki, Nerea Gurrutxaga in collaboration with the dancers.
Concept and project management: Matilda Bilberg
Collaboration partners: Västerås City and Västerås Concert Hall

Reserach Residency at Espace L'Moutalâte

14.10 - 25.10.2024
"Hold On to the Boob: A Practice for (Co-)Existence in Unstable Times" is a call to action. It urges us to reconsider how we interact with one another, how we acknowledge our privileges, and how we can collectively strive for a more just and empathetic world. Through the universal language of dance, we aim to inspire reflection, dialogue, and ultimately, change.
Within this research we aim to question and redefine our role as artists in this rapidly changing world. Our main interest addresses the challenges and opportunities of bringing our work beyond North West Europe in a sustainable and respectful manner. Using dance as a medium to foster empathy, equality, and co-existence we want to explore the idea of building and sustaining projects and networks that promote equality and cultural appreciation.
Our choreographic approach is rooted in the intelligence of the body and the intuitive interplay between performers. We strive to dissolve the boundary between “performance” and “non-performance”, embracing authenticity and equality in movement. By utilizing improvisation, we tap into the power of spontaneity, allowing the moment to shape the dance. We are deeply intrigued by the body’s ability to communicate without words, particularly through touch, exploring its potential as a fundamental element of expression in the context of dance.
Part of our residency in Espace L’Moutalate will be an exchange through a workshop series of three meetings in the center of Dar Bellarj on the topic of “ma danse, ma ville”.
